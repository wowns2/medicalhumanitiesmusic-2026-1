# The Soloist

In the film *The Soloist* (2009), directed by Joe Wright, the use of Ludwig van Beethoven’s Symphony No. 3 in E-flat major, Op. 55 'Eroica' serves as a profound sonic metaphor for Nathaniel Ayers’ internal struggle with schizophrenia. The movie masterfully portrays the music as both a sanctuary and a source of torment; while vibrant visual palettes and grand auditory scales capture the pure ecstasy Nathaniel experiences through Beethoven, these moments are sharply interrupted by the onset of auditory hallucinations. By distorting refined classical melodies into piercing dissonance or abrupt silence, the film illustrates how the disease unravels artistic order, allowing the audience to directly experience the fragmented psychological state of a gifted musician whose world is caught between sublime harmony and neurological chaos.
