# Coda

In *CODA*, music paradoxically depicts hearing impairment through the effective use of 'silence.' During Ruby's climactic performance, the film’s audio cuts out, forcing the audience to experience the performance from the perspective of her deaf family. This creative choice emphasizes that music is not just an auditory experience but an emotional resonance felt through vibration, sign language, and visual expression, ultimately bridging the gap between the hearing and the deaf worlds.
